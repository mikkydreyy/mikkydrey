Early Career and Breakthrough

Wizkid's breakthrough came with the release of his debut album "Superstar" in 2011. The album featured hit singles like "Holla at Your Boy," "Tease Me/Bad Guys," and "Don't Dull," which garnered significant airplay and established him as a leading figure in the Nigerian music scene. "Superstar" won the Best Album of the Year at the 2012 Nigeria Entertainment Awards.

In 2014, Wizkid released his second studio album, "Ayo," which included international hit singles such as "Jaiye Jaiye" featuring Femi Kuti, and the remix of "Ojuelegba," which featured Drake and Skepta. The "Ojuelegba" remix further increased his visibility on the international music stage.

Global Recognition

Wizkid's global recognition soared with his feature on Drake's 2016 hit single "One Dance." The song topped the charts in 15 countries, including the United States, the United Kingdom, Canada, and Australia. It became one of the best-selling digital singles of all time, earning Wizkid his first entry into the Guinness World Records for the most streamed song on Spotify in one week (male).

In 2017, Wizkid released his third studio album, "Sounds from the Other Side," which included collaborations with international artists such as Chris Brown, Ty Dolla Sign, Major Lazer, and Drake. The album marked a significant shift in his sound, blending Afrobeats with Caribbean influences.

Recent Works

In 2020, Wizkid released his highly anticipated album "Made in Lagos," which received critical acclaim and featured artists such as Burna Boy, Skepta, H.E.R., and Damian Marley. The single "Essence," featuring Tems, became a global hit, earning widespread praise and becoming the first Nigerian song to chart on the Billboard Hot 100. A remix featuring Justin Bieber helped propel the song even further into the global spotlight.

Major Awards and Recognitions

  • 2021: Best Music Video for his feature on Beyoncé’s "Brown Skin Girl" from the "Lion King: The Gift" album.

BET Awards

  • 2012: Best International Act: Africa.
  • 2017: Best International Act: Africa.
  • 2020: BET Her Award for "Brown Skin Girl."

  • 2016: Best African Act.
  • 2016: Worldwide Act.

MOBO Awards

  • 2011: Best African Act.
  • 2016: Best African Act.
  • 2020: Best African Act.

Billboard Music Awards

  • 2021: Top R&B Song for his feature on "Essence" (featuring Tems).

NAACP Image Awards

  • 2020: Outstanding Duo, Group or Collaboration (Contemporary) for "Brown Skin Girl."

Soul Train Music Awards

2019: The Ashford and Simpson Songwriter’s Award for "Brown Skin Girl."
  • 2020: Best Collaboration for "Brown Skin Girl."

Additional Achievements

Wizkid has received numerous other awards and nominations from prestigious institutions such as The Headies, Nigeria Entertainment Awards, AFRIMA Awards, and more. His contributions to the global music scene have not only put Nigerian music on the map but have also paved the way for other African artists to gain international recognition.

Wizkid's influence extends beyond music. He has been a prominent voice in advocating for social issues, such as the #EndSARS movement in Nigeria, which protested against police brutality. His global reach and social impact have solidified his status as one of the most influential African artists of his generation.

Collaborations and Impact

Wizkid has collaborated with a wide range of artists across different genres, enhancing his versatility and appeal. Notable collaborations include:

  • "Brown Skin Girl" with Beyoncé, a track that celebrates Black women and has won multiple awards.
  • "One Dance" with Drake, which helped him achieve international stardom.
  • "Come Closer" featuring Drake, which further cemented his place in the global music scene.

His ability to blend different musical styles, from Afrobeats to reggae, and R&B to hip-hop, has made him a trailblazer in bringing African music to the forefront of the global music industry. Wizkid continues to influence the next generation of artists and remains a key figure in the evolution of contemporary African music.
